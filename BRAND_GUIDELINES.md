# Evolution Stables Brand Guidelines

This document defines the voice, tone, and writing standards for all Evolution Stables content.

---

## Core Brand Mandates

### 1. Voice & Tone: Understated Authority
- Be declarative, confident, and calm
- Don't shout innovation; prove it
- State facts definitively without arrogance

### 2. Clarity & Precision
- Every sentence must earn its place
- Prioritise clarity first, poetry second
- Make complexity accessible without oversimplification

### 3. Language: British English
Always use British spelling and conventions:
- colour (not color)
- modernise (not modernize)
- analyse (not analyze)
- organise (not organize)
- favoured, behaviour, honour, etc.

### 4. No Jargon or Hype
Avoid marketing hyperbole and empty superlatives.

### 5. Syntax: Active Voice
Use active voice constructions. Avoid passive voice.

### 6. Audience Balance
Content must work for both:
- Racing purists (heritage, tradition)
- Fintech investors (precision, capability)

### 7. Brand Identity
Evolution Stables represents the intersection of **racing heritage** and **digital infrastructure**.

---

## Writing Principles

- **Precision over Poetry**: Clarity is non-negotiable. Elegance is earned.
- **Confidence without Arrogance**: State facts definitively. Let the work speak.
- **Heritage meets Innovation**: Honour racing tradition while embracing digital transformation.
- **Substance over Style**: Every claim must be backed by capability or evidence.

---

## Forbidden Phrases

❌ **Never use:**
- "Revolutionary" → ✅ Use "redefining" or "reshaping"
- "Game-changing" → ✅ Use "significant" or "material"
- "Disrupting" → ✅ Use "modernising" or "evolving"
- "Cutting-edge" → ✅ Use "contemporary" or "current"
- "Next-generation" → ✅ Use "modern" or "advanced"
- Exclamation marks (except in direct quotes)
- Passive voice constructions
- Marketing superlatives without evidence

## Content Personas

While all content follows the core brand mandates above, different contexts may call for different approaches. These personas represent stylistic variations, not brand deviations.

### James – The Storyteller (Default)
**Style:** Narrative-driven, contextual, engaging  
**Tone:** Warm authority, accessible expertise  

**Use for:**
- Landing page content
- Product descriptions
- Client-facing narratives
- Investment memos

**Characteristics:**
- Connect technical details to human outcomes
- Use analogies from racing heritage
- Build narrative momentum
- Balance warmth with professionalism

**Example:**
> ❌ "The system processes data"  
> ✅ "The system translates raw data into actionable intelligence"

---

### Liam – The Analyst
**Style:** Clinical, data-driven, methodical  
**Tone:** Measured, precise, evidence-based  

**Use for:**
- Performance reports
- Technical documentation
- Investment analysis
- Audit trails

**Characteristics:**
- Prioritise quantifiable statements
- Use specific metrics
- Maintain analytical distance
- Structure information hierarchically

**Example:**
> ❌ "We're excited to announce..."  
> ✅ "The system now provides..."

---

### Clara – The Educator
**Style:** Explanatory, structured, progressive  
**Tone:** Patient authority, teaching-focused  

**Use for:**
- Onboarding content
- Help documentation
- Educational materials
- Walkthroughs

**Characteristics:**
- Break complexity into digestible components
- Use clear hierarchical structure
- Define terms before using them
- Anticipate reader questions

**Example:**
> ❌ "Advanced features"  
> ✅ "Features designed for users who require [specific capability]"

---

### Ethan – The Pundit
**Style:** Opinionated, direct, provocative  
**Tone:** Confident, challenging, unapologetic  

**Use for:**
- Strategy commentary
- Competitive positioning
- Industry analysis
- Opinion pieces

**Characteristics:**
- State positions clearly and early
- Challenge conventional thinking
- Maintain edge without aggression
- Back opinions with demonstrable facts

**Example:**
> ❌ "Possibly the best"  
> ✅ "Demonstrably superior in [specific metric]"

**Note:** Even at maximum confidence, maintain composure. Sharp, not shrill.

## Quick Reference Checklist

Before publishing any content, verify:

- [ ] Uses British English spelling
- [ ] Written in active voice
- [ ] No forbidden phrases or hyperbole
- [ ] Claims backed by evidence or capability
- [ ] Appropriate persona for context
- [ ] Balances technical precision with accessibility
- [ ] Reflects both heritage and innovation
- [ ] Maintains understated authority throughout

## Integration Notes

### For Developers
- Reference these guidelines when writing UI copy, error messages, and tooltips
- Use James (Storyteller) as default for user-facing content
- Use Clara (Educator) for help text and onboarding

### For Content Creators
- All blog posts, articles, and marketing materials must follow core brand mandates
- Choose persona based on content purpose and audience
- When in doubt, default to James

### For Designers
- Typography and visual hierarchy should reinforce understated authority
- Avoid flashy animations or aggressive CTAs
- Let precision and clarity guide design decisions
